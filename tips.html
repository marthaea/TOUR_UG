TIPS!

1. Prepare for Visa Requirements Most visitors need a visa to enter Uganda, which can be applied for online in advance through the eVisa system. Some countries are eligible for a visa on arrival, but it’s best to check current requirements. Consider an East African Tourist Visa if you plan to visit Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda in one trip, as this visa covers all three countries.

2. Get Vaccinated and Take Health Precautions Uganda requires a Yellow Fever vaccination certificate upon entry. It’s also advisable to get vaccinations for Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and Meningitis. Malaria is present in Uganda, so consult a healthcare provider for anti-malarial medication and pack insect repellent to prevent bites.

3. Plan for Changing Weather Uganda has a tropical climate with rainy and dry seasons. The best times to visit are the dry seasons (December to February and June to August) for easier trekking and wildlife viewing. Pack lightweight, breathable clothing for the heat, but also bring a rain jacket and sturdy shoes, especially if you’re trekking in national parks.

4. Bring Cash and Be Mindful of Payment Options The Ugandan Shilling (UGX) is the official currency, and it’s useful to have cash on hand for smaller purchases, as credit cards are not widely accepted in rural areas. ATMs are available in major cities, and U.S. dollars are accepted in many tourist areas. However, ensure you carry new, undamaged U.S. bills, as banks may reject older or torn notes.

Be Ready for Ugandan Cuisine Ugandan food is hearty and features staples like matoke (mashed plantains), posho (maize flour porridge), beans, and roasted meats. Trying local dishes is part of the experience! Tap water is not safe to drink. Stick to bottled or filtered water, which is widely available.

6. Be Respectful of Local Customs and Etiquette Ugandans are very polite, and greetings are important. Take time to greet people respectfully, especially in rural areas. Dress modestly, especially outside of urban areas and in religious sites. Avoid overly revealing clothing as a sign of respect.

7. Be Safe on the Roads Uganda’s road conditions can vary, and traffic is usually dense in cities like Kampala. Consider hiring a local driver, or use ride-hailing services like Uber, which are available in some areas. If you’re renting a vehicle, a 4x4 is recommended for navigating rough terrain, especially if you’re visiting national parks.

8. Respect Wildlife and Follow Park Rules Uganda’s wildlife is incredible, but always maintain a safe distance from animals and follow park guides’ instructions. Gorilla trekking is a must-do, but permits are required and should be booked well in advance. Gorilla trekking is an unforgettable experience but also a respectful interaction with endangered species.

9. Bring Essential Supplies Packing essentials include sunscreen, a wide-brimmed hat, and comfortable clothing suited for hiking or safaris. If you’re going off-grid or trekking, consider bringing portable power banks, as charging stations may not always be available in remote areas.

10. Get Travel Insurance Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, evacuation, and any activities like gorilla trekking or water sports you plan to undertake.
